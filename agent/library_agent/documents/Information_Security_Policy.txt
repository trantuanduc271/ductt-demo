1. Purpose
The purpose of this Information Security Policy is to define the organizationâ€™s commitment and approach to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information assets.
This policy establishes a framework for managing information security risks in alignment with recognized international standards and regulatory obligations.
2. Scope
This policy applies to:
All information assets created, processed, stored, or transmitted by the organization
All systems, networks, applications, and infrastructure, whether on-premises or cloud-based
All personnel, including employees, contractors, temporary staff, and third-party users
3. Policy Statement
The organization shall maintain an Information Security Management System (ISMS) to systematically identify, assess, and manage information security risks.
Information security controls shall be designed to:
Protect sensitive and confidential information from unauthorized access or disclosure
Ensure data accuracy and integrity
Maintain system availability in line with business requirements
4. Governance and Responsibilities
4.1 Executive Management
Endorse and support the Information Security Policy
Ensure adequate resources are allocated to information security
4.2 Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)
Own and maintain the ISMS
Define security policies, standards, and procedures
Oversee security risk assessments and incident response
4.3 Employees and Contractors
Comply with all information security policies and procedures
Complete mandatory security awareness training
Report suspected security incidents promptly
5. Information Security Controls
5.1 Access Control
Access to systems and data shall be granted on the principle of least privilege.
User access rights must be approved by the appropriate data or system owner.
Access shall be reviewed at least annually and upon role change or termination.
5.2 Asset Management
Information assets shall be inventoried and assigned an owner.
Assets must be classified according to the organizationâ€™s data classification scheme.
5.3 Acceptable Use
Organizational systems may only be used for authorized business purposes.
The installation of unauthorized software or tools is prohibited.
5.4 Incident Management
All suspected or confirmed security incidents must be reported without delay.
Security incidents shall be managed according to the Incident Response Procedure.
5.5 Business Continuity
Information systems supporting critical business processes shall be included in business continuity and disaster recovery planning.
6. Awareness and Training
All personnel must complete annual information security awareness training and acknowledge this policy.
7. Compliance and Enforcement
Non-compliance with this policy may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination of employment or contract.
8. Review
This policy shall be reviewed annually or following significant changes to business operations, technology, or regulatory requirements.
